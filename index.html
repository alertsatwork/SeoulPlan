At a glance

Day A · stream, gates & hanok loop
1Cheonggye Plaza & stream›
~10:00 · 60–75 min · open 24h · ~10 min walk from base
Head of the restored stream. Drop below street level and walk east beside the water — stepping-stone crossings, shade, occasional art.
Open in Naver
2DDPoptional · quick›
~11:30 · 30–45 min · 10:00–20:00
Zaha Hadid's flowing silver landmark at the east end of the walk. Architecture and a photo; free galleries inside if you want more.
Some find the interior under-used and the design shops pricey — hence a quick look.
Open in Naver
3Heunginjimun — East Gate›
~12:20 · 20–30 min · 3-min walk from DDP
The "Great East Gate," a Joseon-era National Treasure with a distinctive semicircular defensive wall curving around its front. Viewed from outside; a short stretch of old city wall sits in the park behind.
Open in Naver
4Gwangjang Market — lunchpick one / both›
~13:00 · 45–60 min · on the stream, west of DDP
Lively old food market — mung-bean pancakes, mayak gimbap, tteokbokki, made in front of you. The kid-friendly counterpart to café-hopping, right on the stream path back toward base.
Open in Naver
5Ikseon-dong + dessertpick one / both›
~14:15 · 45–75 min · by Jongno 3-ga
Maze of restored 1920s hanok turned into cafés and small shops. Adult-leaning — for kids it's a dessert-and-explore stop.
Pick the busiest-looking bingsu (shaved-ice) hanok café in the central lane; order one big bowl to share and ask for extra spoons up front. If one has a queue, the next alley has another.
Open in Naver
Day B · markets, shopping & a view
6Namdaemun Market›
~11:00 · 90–120 min · Sun 11:00–17:00
Seoul's biggest, oldest market — clothing, souvenirs, kitchenware, and the famous kalguksu (knife-cut noodle) alley for a cheap, hot lunch.
Open in Naver
7Sungnyemun — South Gate›
~13:00 · 20 min · Tue–Sun 09:00–18:00 (closed Mon)
National Treasure No. 1, built 1398 as the walled city's main southern entrance. A short walk from the market — walk through the arch into the grounds, free; lovely lit at night.
Open in Naver
8Myeongdong — shops & food›
~13:30 · 2–3 hr · 15-min walk / one stop
K-beauty, fashion and big stores: Lotte & Shinsegae department stores, the 12-floor Daiso, Olive Young. Street-food carts (corn dogs, tteokbokki, hotteok) build up through the afternoon.
Open in Naver
9N Seoul Toweroptional · sunset›
~17:30 · 2 hr · open to ~22:30 · cable car near Myeongdong
Namsan cable car up to the love-lock fences and 360° views — best at sunset. Low-commitment; decide on the day by weather and energy.
One evening highlight per day: do the tower here and put the cruise on Day A; skip it and the cruise becomes Day B's finish.
Open in Naver
Floats · attach to either day
10Han River cruiseoptional · evening›
~60–90 min · evening best · from Yeouido or Ttukseom
Sit-down sightseeing along the river past the lit skyline and bridges. Riverside parks at the docks are good for a pre-cruise stroll or a convenience-store picnic.
Confirm the dock (Yeouido vs Ttukseom) when you book — operators and times vary.
Open in Naver

How to mix & match

Keep the un-marked stops and you have a working day; "optional" ones move or drop freely.

Day A finish (4 & 5): Gwangjang and Ikseon-dong sit minutes apart — do both, or pick one.

The two view stops (9 & 10) never share a day.

Both gates are in: East Gate on Day A, South Gate on Day B.

Day A is walkable end-to-end; Day B is one subway stop or 15-min walk from Euljiro 3-ga.

Times are suggestions, not rails · photos load when online
