Base Euljiro 3-ga — your start point
Both days radiate from here. Day A you can do almost entirely on foot along the stream; Day B is one subway stop or a 15-minute walk west.
Day A Stream, gates & hanok loop

Flat, mostly on foot. Walk the restored stream east, glance at the architecture, see the East Gate, eat, loop back for food-market grazing and dessert. Cheonggyecheon is loveliest lit up — this day also works as an evening.

Anchor Cheonggye Plaza & stream
청계광장 / 청계천
~10:00 · 60–75 min · open 24h
The head of the Cheonggyecheon. Drop below street level and walk east beside the water — stepping-stone crossings, shade, the odd art installation.

Anchor · gate Heunginjimun (East Gate)
흥인지문 (동대문)
~12:20 · 20–30 min · 3-min walk from DDP
The "Great East Gate," a Joseon-era National Treasure, distinctive for the semicircular defensive wall curving around its front. Viewed from outside; a short stretch of the old city wall sits in the park behind.

Swappable Gwangjang Market — lunch & grazing
광장시장
~13:00 · 45–60 min · on the stream, west of DDP
Lively old food market — mung-bean pancakes, mayak gimbap, tteokbokki, made in front of you. The kid-friendly counterpart to café-hopping; sits right on the stream path back toward base.

Swappable Ikseon-dong + dessert
익선동 한옥거리
~14:15 · 45–75 min · by Jongno 3-ga
Maze of restored 1920s hanok turned into cafés and small shops. Adult-leaning — for the kids it's a dessert-and-explore stop, not an activity.Pick the busiest-looking bingsu (shaved-ice) hanok café in the central lane; order one big bowl to share and ask for extra spoons up front. If one has a queue, the next alley has another.

Day B Markets, shopping & a view

Self-contained, one stop from base, building toward an optional sunset. Go early — Namdaemun is quieter on Sundays and many stalls shut by late afternoon.

Anchor Namdaemun Market
남대문시장
~11:00 · 90–120 min · Sun 11:00–17:00
Seoul's biggest, oldest market — clothing, souvenirs, kitchenware, and the famous kalguksu (knife-cut noodle) alley for a cheap, hot lunch.

Anchor · gate Sungnyemun (South Gate)
숭례문 (남대문)
~13:00 · 20 min · Tue–Sun 09:00–18:00 (closed Mon)
National Treasure No. 1, built 1398 as the walled city's main southern entrance. A short walk from the market — you can walk through the arch into the grounds, free; lovely lit at night.

How to mix & match

  • Anchors hold each day's shape — keep at least the anchors and you have a working day.
  • Swappable (Day A finish): Gwangjang and Ikseon-dong sit minutes apart, so do both — graze savoury at Gwangjang, then dessert at Ikseon-dong — or drop either.
  • The two view stops never share a day. Tower on Day B → cruise on Day A. Skip the tower → cruise ends Day B.
  • Both gates are in: East Gate on Day A (by DDP), South Gate on Day B (by Namdaemun market).
  • Day A is walkable end-to-end; Day B is one subway stop or a 15-min walk from Euljiro 3-ga.
  • Maps: Google won't navigate in Korea — use the Naver or Kakao links. If a pin looks wrong, search the Korean name shown on each card.
